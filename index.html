How easy is it for students to reach school by transit or bike?

This project was planned to examine public transportation and bike path accessibility for high school, college, and university students in urban areas of San Bernardino County. Using Metrolink, Omnitrans bus routes, and bikeway data, the maps highlight where access is strong and where gaps exist for public school students.

Project Goals

  • Visualize public transit and bike-path access around high schools, colleges, and universities.
  • Identify areas where students may lack convenient transportation options.
  • Support planning discussions about equity and mobility for youth in San Bernardino County.

Study Area & Population

The focus is on urbanized parts of San Bernardino County, with an emphasis on public high schools, colleges, and university campuses. The key layers include school locations, Metrolink stations, Omnitrans bus routes/stops, bike paths, and supporting demographic data related to student populations.

This site integrates ArcGIS Online maps, a Leaflet map, and a StoryMap to focus on public mobility for adult students in the San Bernardino Area.
